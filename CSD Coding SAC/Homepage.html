Box Hill

How it was named
Box Hill was named simply for the high preponderance of yellow box trees in the area growing amongst the local forest. It was named at a local residents meeting in 1861.

Bulleen

How it was named
The name Bulleen comes from the aboriginal word for ‘lyrebird’. It was named after the nearby Bolin Bolin billabong.

Doncaster

How it was named
The suburb's name comes from innkeeper John Robert Wilson, who owned the Doncaster Arms Hotel, which drew its name from Doncaster in Yorkshire.

Donvale

How it was named
Donvale was said to be named after the intersection of the two major roads going through the suburb – Doncaster Rd and Springvale Rd.

Templestowe

How it was named
The village of Templestowe was surveyed in 1852. Two theories have been offered to explain the derivation of its name. One suggests it derived from Sir Walter Scott's novel Ivanhoe. The second is that it originated from a small English settlement called Temple's Stowe.
